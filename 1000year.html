Resources

  • Stave Church (wikipedia) - Wooden churches in Norway that are 800+ years old.
    • NYT article on stave churches - out of 800+ built, 32 remain
  • How Buildings Learn (book)
  • Hope for Architecture - A vernacular masonry architecture designed for multi century lifespans
  • Hindu Temples, which are often designed for extremely long lifespans. I need to dive into this more, everything I've found is heavy on spiritual principles and light on construction techniques.
    • Monolith Foundation: Built to Last 1000 years - Foundation for a Hindu temple in Hawaii. No rebar, very slow-curing mix.
    • Trivedi Group - Group that builds many Hindu temples
    • Devalaya Vastu - Design principles of Hindu temples.
    • Vastu Shastra (wiki) - Traditional Hindu temple architecture
    • Brihadisvara Temple - Built around 1000 AD. Made of Granite
    • Which is the oldest Hindu temple which still exists? (quora) - there are a lot of these that are 1500 years old or older. Seem to be mostly made of granite.
  • Japanese temple rebuilt every 20 years - recurring theme is that it's the buildings that remain in use that survive best
  • Does lumber grow stronger with age? - TLDR: it does as it loses moisture, but other aging factors reduce strength. In general: no
  • Roman Pantheon (wiki)
  • List of oldest known surviving buildings (wiki) - the older you get, the more the buildings resemble a pile of rocks. They're also mostly tombs, which contradicts the idea that structures should be kept in use. Fewer of these are in the desert than I would have expected.
    • Ruwanwelisaya (wiki) example of something that has "survived" by being rebuilt frequently
